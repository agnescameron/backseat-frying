pancita

(mexican tripe soup)

tripe hype
  • a sheet or 2 of tripe (I like book tripe, honeycomb is good too)
  • 2 or 3 bay leaves
  • stock of some kind (if you do this properly, 2 pig's feet)
  • 1 small (sweet, if poss) onion
  • garlic garlic
  • 2 or 3 dry guajillo chillies

  • coriander leaves
  • lime
  1. First, you need to deal with the tripe smell (and, if using pig feet, the pork smell too). Put both in a large pot, and bring to the boil. Go hard for 10min, then drain and slice the tripe. I tend to slice the tripe into long, thin pieces when using book tripe, and chunkier half-inch sections when using honeycomb.
  2. Put the tripe (+ feet) back in the pan with bay leaves, and bring to a simmer. If no feet, add the stock here. (this is the part where I added squash chunks too)
  3. Soak the dried chillies in water till soft. Blend, along with 1/2 the onion, cumin, garlic and salt, into a potent red paste. Add to the soup, and simmer for about a half hour. Season with salt and oregano.
  4. Serve topped with the rest of the onion, diced, coriander leaves and a squeeze of lime.
This soup is complicated and spicy and chewy and smoky, and goes well with simple things like fried fish, boiled eggs, boiled potatoes and fried greens. Last time I made this, I added a chunked acorn squash, which added a nice sweet flavour to the smoky chillies. Haven't made this with the pig foot stock yet, but I imagine it to be delicious.
