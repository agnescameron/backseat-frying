hiyayakko

like a japanese savory flan, there's something that feels very luxurious and a bit executive about this dish, but it's also very simple and fast. a great 'work lunch' to really treat yourself in the middle of the day.
  • chilled silken tofu (gotta be silky)

  • ginger
  • tbsp soy sauce (ideally japanese)
  • spring onions
  • dashi powder

  • katsuobushi (optional)

  • mirin (/pinch of sugar)

  1. finely mince the ginger (kind of paste-like) and stir with soy, a pinch of dashi powder and mirin/sugar.
  2. cut a generous cuboid of the silken tofu, and dress with finely sliced spring onions (some fancy people like to slice them lengthways), and the katsuobushi if using. pour over the soy/dashi/ginger and serve
one of the things that really makes this dish is really peeling and mincing the ginger, and very finely chopping the spring onions: the tofu is so delicate that you don't want to hurt it with big chunks of onion, or bits of ginger skin. it's also great with sliced avocado on top.
