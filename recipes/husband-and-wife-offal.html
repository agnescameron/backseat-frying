husband and wife offal slices

fuqi feipian 夫妻肺片

this has been modified to suit current tripe enthusiasm (e.g. it's meant to have ingredients other than tripe), but what it lacks in authenticity it makes up for in tripe. thank us later
  • beef tripe (I like book tripe, honeycomb more trad here)
  • lump of ginger
  • 1 star anise
  • tsp sichuan peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 spring onions
  • shaoxing wine
  • (if you have it) cassia bark, sand ginger
  • stock (ideally beef, I used chicken)
  • a little celery
  • chilli oil
  • sesame seeds
  • peanuts
  • coriander leaves
  1. boil the tripe on high heat for 10min to disperse the tripey flavour. rinse, then cut into very fine slices (if cutting book tripe, do so perpendicular to the direction of the folds so you get nice fronds).
  2. while boiling the tripe, start a stock. Mix the stock with ginger, star anise, 1/2 of the sichuan peppercorns, black peppercorns, fennel seeds, spring onions, shaoxing, cassia bark and sand ginger. bring to a simmer. add in the sliced tripe and leave to cook for 20min.
  3. fry the peanuts in a pan till brown. dry-fry and grind up the rest of the sichuan peppercorn.
  4. once the tripe has cooked, move it onto a plate and arrange it. top with minced celery, chilli oil, ground peppercorns, sesame, peanuts and coriander leaves. Spoon over the remaining stock, and serve cold or warm.
This is normally made with beef shin slices too, so you get tripe-shin-tripe in layers. A delicious aperitif!
