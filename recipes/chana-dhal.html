chana dhal

slow salad for the cold winter
  • a few cups of chana dhal (yellow split peas)
  • 1 onion
  • garlic
  • ginger
  • chillies
  • coriander (leaves)
  • 1/2 tsp coriander (seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin (seeds)
  • 1-2 tomatoes (optional)
  • turmeric
  • chilli powder
  • garam masala
  1. if you're feeling organised, put the chana dhal in a big bowl of water to soak for a couple of hours. this isn't essential, but will speed up the cooking a lot.
  2. put the chana dhal in a big pan, and cover with twice as much water, then a lid. add in 1/2-1tsp turmeric, and bring to the boil. (this bit could be done much faster in a pressure cooker)
  3. turn down the heat, and simmer the dhal until soft. if you didn't soak this part takes about 1 hour.
  4. now to make the tarka: very finely chop the onions, and fry over a low heat till translucent
  5. add in chopped ginger and garlic, fry some more, then add cumin coriander chilli powder and garam masala. fry fry fry, then add in the tomatoes
  6. once the tomatoes have cooked down, stir the tarka into the dhal. add salt to taste
this recipe also works well with red lentils, which are a lot faster to cook (about 20 mins, rather than an hour). The chana dhal have a really lovely nutty taste though, and maintain their shape and texture a bit better when cooked, so if you have the time I think it's worth it. serve with white rice and a pickle!
