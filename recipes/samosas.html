samosas

∆ spicy wedding triangles ∆
∆ congratulations, alison and luiza! ∆
∆ makes 25 ∆
  • spring roll wrappers / filo pastry
    (if you're in egg house we left some in the freezer)


  • 2 big potatoes
  • half a bag frozen green peas
  • 3 green chillies
  • 1 sweet onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • black onion seeds
  • cumin seeds
  • garam masala
  • much garlic
  • mustard seeds
  1. put the potatoes in the microwave / a pan of salted boiling water and cook till soft and mashable
  2. heat oil in a pan, and toast the whole spices. when fragrant, add in the garlic, then add the chopped onions when fragrant and fry on a low heat
  3. add in the chopped carrot and turmeric and garam masala, and fry till soft and translucent
  4. roughly mash the potatoes. stir in with the green peas into the onions and carrots, add salt to taste (quite a bit)
  5. as the mixture cools a bit, break out the wrappers. this guide
    6. is pretty good for folding with square wrappers
  6. get a deepish pan, and fill with 1.5in or so of veg oil. heat till it gets pretty hot, then turn the heat down so it maintains the temperature. fry a couple of samosas at a time till they're brown and crispy. put on kitchen towel to drain the excess oil
spring roll wrappers are a bit easier, and more sturdy to transport. the filo ones get very crispy though, and while less authentic are amazing eaten straight out the pan. we made 45 of these for alison and luiza's wedding, and they went pretty quick!

These guys are vegan, but we think it would be really good with beef/lamb!
