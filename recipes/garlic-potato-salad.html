garlic potato salad

we ate this a lot in palestine. its name in arabic is كالثوم (kalthum), which literally means 'garlicky'. it's stretching 'salad' a bit and it might sound like a lot to eat this with bread, but it's also delicious
  • white potatoes (more floury than waxy is best)
  • olive oil
  • salt
  • garlic (at least 6 cloves)
  • sumac (optional)
  • parsley (optional)
  1. boil the potatoes in a pan till they're soft, and crumble when you poke them with a fork. you probably don't need to peel them: just scrub them hard before you put them in to thin the skin.
  2. mash with salt, oil and minced garlic. go for it with the olive oil, as it gives a lot of flavour. Top with sumac and/or minced parsley, and another drizzle of olive oil.
really good served with flatbread, and accompanied by other, more salady salads.
