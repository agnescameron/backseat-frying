dumplings

if one breaks, gary will know
  • 1lb ground beef (or pork, shrimp)
  • dumpling wrappers

  • small handful finely diced spring onions
  • a good glug light soy sauce (for flavor but don’t turn the mix brown)

  • salt to taste (lick the raw mince a little bit after mixing)
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1 tbsp garlic
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1tsp sesame oil
  • 2tbsp veg oil
  • (optional) finely chopped shiitake mushroom
  • (optional) finely chopped chinese cabbage (you need to salt this first and leave 30min to draw the water out)
  1. mix the mix using utensils then scrunching everything with your hand until it squeezes out between your fingers. get out dumpling wrappers, spoon mixture in, and seal tightly (don’t over stuff, might help to watch a video on how to fold).
  2. once all are ready, bring a pan of water to the boil. do a batch at a time, leaving space so they don’t stick to each other. As they drop into the pan, stir the water so they swirl gently around each other. Cook for the length of time it takes the pan to come back to the boil after you add a cup of cold water 3 times. Do not let them break.
  3. dip in soy, shenxiang vinegar, sesame oil, laoganma — can also eat with raw garlic.

dumpling soup

After having dumplings, ladle the cloudy dough broth into a bowl with some spring onions and some of the soy sauce used for dipping.
