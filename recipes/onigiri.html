onigiri

(rice ball)

the balls:
  • furikake

  • cooked white rice (if you use leftover, don't fridge it cause it dries out!)
  • dashi powder

  • mirin (/pinch of sugar)


possible fillings:
  • umeboshi

  • spicy tuna: kewpie mayo, tinned tuna, gochujang
  • salmon+kewpie mayo
  • kimchi
  • seaweed salad
  1. take the rice, and mix with the mirin, and a pinch of dashi powder. for this to work, the rice should be soft and sticky!
  2. get a small piece of cling film (or just wet your hands), and pat about a tbsp of rice onto it. put a bit of filling in the centre, then add more rice on top. gently pack into a triangle shape
  3. when you're ready to serve, sprinkle with furikake, and eat wrapped in a strip of nori! if you're taking this as a packed lunch, pack the nori separately, or it'll get too soggy.
If you have these leftover, heat some oil in a hot pan and fry each side (and the edges) until crisp and dark brown. These make a great travelling snack, I'd take a tea egg too!
